Church of Saint Sava, Belgrade

It is located on the Vračar plateau, on the eastern part of the Svetosavski Trg square in Belgrade which is believed to be the location where the remains of Saint Sava were burned in 1595 by Ottoman Grand Vizier Sinan Pasha in order to break the Serbian spirit.

Church of Saint Sava is the not only the largest Serbian Orthodox church it is the largest Orthodox place of worship in the Balkans and one of the largest Orthodox churches in the world.

Its total height reaches an astounding 82 m with the dome being 70 m high and the main gold plated cross giving it an additional 12 m height. The central dome is 4,000 ton heavy and its lifting was the greatest achievement of the construction process.
